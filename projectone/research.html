Project 1: Instruction Sets for Strangers

Collaborator: Kevin Lin

Location

For this project, Kevin and I decided to go to Pier 1, Riverside Park. The pier looks out to the Hudson River and it overlooks the New Jersey skyline. It is a beautiful public space for people to take a stroll, walk their pups, do some fishing, exercise or just relax. I love coming here because it is very quiet and I love hearing the waves crashing onto the pier. Prior to our outing, we did a quick research on the site and we found out that it used to be a part of the abandoned Penn Central Railyard. Today, it is a beautiful pier for people to enjoy.

Research and Brainstorming

AEIOU

For our first research, we did the AEIOU exerecise. We sat on one of the benches at the pier, observed some strangers and note down a few things that stood out. It was really exciting to see the diverse activities that are happening on the pier. There were some people fishing, sunbathing, flying kites, resting after an exercise, taking a stroll with their partner, cycling, etc.

Behaviorial Mapping

Additionally, we did a behaviorial mapping on some of the users on the pier. One thing we noticed is that most people walk all the way down to the end of the pier. For those who are exercising or on a call, they will immediately turn back, but for those who are resting or just taking a stroll, most of them would find an empty bench.
